How to Reduce you Carbon Footprint


Climate change is real, it's scary, overwhelming - but not unsolavable.

Greenhouse gases have far-reaching environmental and health effects. This is why it is crucial to keep them in track. While real solutions will require action on a global scale, there are choices you can make in your day-to-day life to lessen your personal impact on the environment.

Here are some helpful tips on how to reduce your personal CO2 Footprint


Go Alternative!

One of the biggest producers of CO2 emissions is the meat industry.
On average, livestock contributes to 7,100 MtCO2e / year or 14.5% of global GHG emissions.
By cutting down on meat and dairy - you can reduce your CO2 footprint significantly.
There are many great alternatives that you could try, including - but not limited to: Tempeh, Tofu, Soy and Oat products.

Reduce-Reuse-Recycle!

Avoid plastic! Do you really need that 10cent bag to bag your 3 items?
Reduce your plastic use by re-using old shopping bags, buy loose fruits and vegetables, and jump on the reusable straw and bottle train!
Check out these sustainable bottles and reusable straws

Shop Local & Seasonal!

One of biggest factors that contributes to carbon emissions of products is the supply chain.
Shopping local and seasonal not only will reduce your carbon footprint, but will also supports your local farmers!
